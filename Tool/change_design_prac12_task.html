Our task is to find out the given moral foundation and actor-target polarity role of a provided text are correct or not. If given annotation is correct check 'yes', otherwise check 'no'. If you checked 'no', then you need to find out the moral foundation of the given text, corresponding to one of six moral principles (e.g., "I give to the poor" expresses care), and then highlight the entities in the text according to (1) their roles - actor (a 'do-er') whose actions influence the target (the 'do-ee'), and (2) polarity, depending on the positive or negative influence of these actions. For example, "I give to the poor", "I" is a positive actor, and "the poor" is a positive target (benefiting from the actor's actions). On the other hand "We are suffering from pandemic" expresses harm as moral principles where "pandemic" is a negative actor, and "we" is a negative target (suffering from the actor's actions).

Overview of the task:
  1. Read the text carefully.
  2. Provide your judgement regarding the moral foundation and actor-target polarity role the text using checkbox mentioning either 'yes' or 'no'.
  3. Select moral foundation of the text using checkbox . You can see the definition of each moral foundation by hovering mouse on them. If the text does not make any moral judgement, check "none". For this case, you don't have to highlight actor-target polarity.
  4. After selecting any moral foundation other than "none", text highlighting for actor-target role with polarity will be visible below. If you select a moral foundation other than "none", you must highlight actor-target polarity.
  5. Choose the Positive Actor / Positive Target / Negative Actor / Negative Target label to highlight the text with the color of the selected label. You can see the definition of actor-target-polarity role by hovering mouse on them.
  6. Highlight words, phrases, or sections of the text for actor-target role with polarity of corresponding moral foundation.
  7. If you made any mistake in highlighting, to unhighlight the previously highlighted text, select Unhighlight.
  8. Finally, click "Submit" button to submit the task.
For selecting Moral Foundation, please look into following categories:
  1. Care/harm: If you feel the text expresses concerns about someone (out-group) other than the speaker being harmed or deserving care, select care/harm as its moral foundation.
  2. Fairness/cheating: If you feel the text expresses justice, rights, and autonomy, comparison to other groups, select fairness/cheating as its moral foundation.
  3. Loyalty/betrayal: If you feel the text expresses an attitude related to "one for all, and all for one", patriotism and self-sacrifice for the group, select loyalty/betrayal as its moral foundation.
  4. Authority/subversion: If you feel the text expresses deference (or opposition) to legitimate authority, hierarchical relationships, traditions, social order, role-based duties, select authority/subversion as its moral foundation.
  5. Sanctity/degradation: If you feel the text expresses physical and spiritual contagion, including virtues of chastity, wholesomeness, and control of desires, select sanctity/degradation as its moral foundation.
  6. Liberty/oppression: If you feel the text expresses feelings of people towards those who dominate them and restrict their liberty, to oppose or take down the oppressor, select liberty/oppression as its moral foundation.
  7. None: If you feel the text does not make any moral judgment (e.g., it’s a fact without explicit indication of moral judgement), or the moral foundation of the text does not fall into any of the above categories, please select "none".
    If the author of the text criticizes the moral judgement expressed by other people (calling out other people's view), please select "none". For example "the people who say 'the vaccine will turn you into a monster' are stupid!" -- in this text "the people" express the moral judgement (Sanctity), but the author is not accepting this judgement.
    If author is praising others in the text (i.e., "The staff at the medical centre are operating such a quick and efficient clinic"), it doesn't have any moral judgment rather than positive sentiment. If the entity of the text has authority figure i.e., "The staff at the medical centre are operating such a quick and efficient clinic, more people should listen to them", it expresses moral judgement (Authority).
    If author express concern but no moral judgement (i.e., "I’m very concerned the Delta variant could mutate into resistant strain as it rapidly replicates in unvaccinated host."), please select "none".
For highlighting actor and target roles with polarity, please look into following definitions:

The roles correspond to actor and target. An actor, a 'do-er', whose actions or influence result in a positive or negative outcome for the target (the 'do-ee'). For each moral foundation, we can associate "entity doing good/bad" (positive and negative actor) and "entity benefiting/suffering" (positive and negative target). It's possible that there could be multiple actors and/or multiple targets. Also, either actor or target could be "None".
Entities can correspond to specific individuals or groups (e.g., all Democrats, people of a given demographic etc.), organizations (e.g., political parties, CDC, FDA, companies etc.), legislation or other political actions (e.g., demonstrations, petitions etc.), disease or natural disasters (e.g., Covid, global warming etc.), scientific or technological innovations (e.g., vaccine, social media, the Internet etc.).
